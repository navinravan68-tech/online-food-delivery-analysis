Online Food Delivery Analysis – Column-wise Insights
1. Customer Gender
Insight:
•	The customer base is almost evenly distributed among Male (24.90%), Female (25.38%), Other (25.01%), and Unknown (24.70%). 
•	No single gender dominates the platform, indicating a diverse customer base. 
•	Marketing campaigns can be designed for a broad audience instead of targeting one specific gender. 
________________________________________
2. City
Insight:
•	Order distribution across Bangalore, Chennai, Delhi, Hyderabad, Mumbai, and Unknown is nearly equal (≈16–17% each). 
•	Customer demand is well balanced across all operating cities. 
•	The business is not dependent on a single geographical market, reducing regional business risk. 
________________________________________
3. Area
Insight:
•	Orders are evenly distributed across North, South, East, West, Central, and Unknown areas. 
•	No area experiences significantly higher or lower demand. 
•	Delivery resources appear to be allocated uniformly across service regions. 
________________________________________
4. Cuisine Type
Insight:
•	Indian cuisine has the highest share (~16.9%), while Italian has the lowest (~16.2%). 
•	Differences between cuisine preferences are minimal. 
•	Customer preferences are well diversified, suggesting restaurants from all cuisine categories contribute similarly to platform demand. 
________________________________________
5. Cancellation Reason
Insight:
•	Nearly 85% of orders are successfully completed (Not Cancelled). 
•	Among cancelled orders: 
o	Unknown reason contributes the largest share (~6%). 
o	Late delivery (~3.1%) is the leading identified operational issue. 
o	Customer cancellation (~3%) and restaurant issues (~3%) occur at similar rates. 
•	Reducing delivery delays can further decrease order cancellations. 
________________________________________
6. Delivery Rating
Insight:
•	Delivery ratings are uniformly distributed between 1 and 5. 
•	Median rating is approximately 3. 
•	There are no significant outliers, indicating consistent customer feedback collection. 
•	Customer satisfaction varies considerably, suggesting opportunities to improve delivery quality. 
________________________________________
7. Delivery Time (Minutes)
Insight:
•	Delivery times range from approximately 20 to 300 minutes. 
•	Median delivery time is around 120 minutes. 
•	The distribution is positively skewed, with many deliveries completed quickly and a smaller number experiencing long delays. 
•	Long delivery durations highlight opportunities to optimize logistics and routing. 
________________________________________
8. Distance (km)
Insight:
•	Delivery distances vary between 1 km and 40 km. 
•	Median delivery distance is approximately 10 km. 
•	Most orders are delivered within shorter distances, while long-distance deliveries occur less frequently. 
•	Long-distance orders may require dynamic pricing or optimized rider allocation. 
________________________________________
9. Discount Applied
Insight:
•	Discounts are concentrated around fixed values (0, 20, 50, 100, and 300). 
•	The median discount is approximately ₹50. 
•	A few high discounts (₹300) appear as outliers, indicating promotional campaigns or special offers. 
•	Most orders receive moderate discounts, helping balance customer acquisition and profitability. 
________________________________________
10. Final Amount
Insight:
•	Final payable amounts range from ₹0 to ₹5000. 
•	Median order value is approximately ₹1800. 
•	Most orders fall within the low-to-mid price range, while premium orders are comparatively fewer. 
•	Revenue is primarily generated from frequent medium-value transactions rather than expensive individual orders. 
________________________________________
11. Delivery Performance
Insight:
•	63.02% of orders experienced delayed deliveries. 
•	32.95% were delivered quickly. 
•	Only 4.03% achieved average delivery performance. 
•	Delivery efficiency is the biggest operational challenge for the platform.
